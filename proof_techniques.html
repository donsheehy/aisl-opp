Proof Techniques

Summary

Proof techniques are the standard methods for establishing the truth of mathematical statements. Part of Logic and Proofs.

  • Direct proof
    • Assume the hypothesis and derive the conclusion step by step.
  • Proof by contrapositive
    • Prove $\neg Q \rightarrow \neg P$ as an equivalent substitute for establishing $P \rightarrow Q$.
  • Proof by contradiction
    • Assume $\neg P$ and derive a logical contradiction, thereby establishing $P$.
  • Proof by cases
    • Split the hypothesis into exhaustive cases and prove the conclusion in each case.
  • Constructive vs. non-constructive proofs
    • A constructive proof exhibits a witness or algorithm that produces the desired object.
    • A non-constructive proof shows existence without producing a specific example.
  • Existence and uniqueness proofs
    • An existence proof produces or proves there is some object satisfying property $P$.
    • A uniqueness proof assumes two such objects exist and shows they must be equal.
  • Counterexamples and disproof
    • One counterexample suffices to refute a universal claim.
