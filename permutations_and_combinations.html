Permutations and Combinations

Summary

Permutations and combinations are the counts of ordered and unordered selections from a finite set. Part of Combinatorics. Builds on basic_counting.

  • $k$-permutations
    • A $k$-permutation of $n$ elements is an ordered selection of $k$ items from $n$ without repetition.
    • The count is $P(n,k) = \dfrac{n!}{(n-k)!}$.
  • Binomial coefficients
    • A combination (or $k$-subset) is an unordered selection of $k$ items from $n$.
    • The count is $\binom{n}{k} = \dfrac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$.
  • Permutations with repetition
    • The number of length-$k$ sequences over an alphabet of size $n$ is $n^k$.
    • The number of distinct permutations of a multiset with multiplicities $n_1, \ldots, n_r$ is $\dfrac{n!}{n_1! \cdots n_r!}$.
  • Combinations with repetition (multisets / stars and bars)
    • The number of multisets of size $k$ drawn from $n$ types is $\binom{n+k-1}{k}$.
