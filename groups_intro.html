Groups (Intro)

Summary

A group is a set $G$ equipped with a binary operation that is associative, has an identity element, and in which every element has an inverse. Part of Algebraic Structures.

  • Definitions and small examples
    • A group $(G, \ast)$ requires: associativity of $\ast$, an identity $e$, and an inverse $a^{-1}$ for each $a \in G$.
    • Standard examples include $(\mathbb{Z}, +)$, $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, +)$ (see modular_arithmetic), $({1,-1}, \cdot)$, and $(GL_n(\mathbb{R}), \cdot)$.
  • Cyclic groups and permutation groups
    • A cyclic group is generated by a single element; $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ and $\mathbb{Z}$ are the only cyclic groups up to isomorphism.
    • A permutation group is a subgroup of $S_n$, the symmetric group on $n$ letters; $|S_n| = n!$.
    • Cayley's theorem states that every group is isomorphic to a permutation group.
