Inclusion–Exclusion Principle

Summary

The inclusion–exclusion principle gives a formula for the size of a finite union in terms of the sizes of intersections. Part of Combinatorics.

  • Statement and proof
    • The formula alternates signs over all nonempty subcollections of the sets being united.
    • The principle is typically proved by induction on the number of sets, or via indicator functions.
  • Derangements
    • The number of derangements of $[n]$ (permutations with no fixed points) is \[ D_n = n!\sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{(-1)^k}{k!}, \] and $D_n / n! \to 1/e$.
  • Surjection counting
    • The number of surjections from $[n]$ to $[k]$ is $\displaystyle\sum_{j=0}^{k} (-1)^j \binom{k}{j} (k-j)^n$.

\[ \left|\bigcup_{i=1}^{n} A_i\right| = \sum_{\emptyset \neq S \subseteq [n]} (-1)^{|S|+1} \left|\bigcap_{i \in S} A_i\right|. \]
