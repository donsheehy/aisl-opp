Free Online Resources for Discrete Mathematics

Summary

This note is a curated list of free, openly licensed discrete mathematics textbooks and courses. Textbooks are prioritized; supplementary courses follow. See discrete_math_outline for the topic map.

Open-Access Textbooks

Discrete Mathematics: An Open Introduction — Oscar Levin

A widely adopted CC BY-SA textbook aimed at first/second-year math and CS undergraduates. Covers logic, proofs, graph theory, counting, sequences, induction, sets, functions, generating functions, and number theory. Over 750 exercises with many solutions/hints.

  • HTML (4th ed.): https://discrete.openmathbooks.org/
  • PDF (4th ed.): https://discrete.openmathbooks.org/pdfs/dmoi4.pdf
  • Runestone interactive (4th ed.): https://runestone.academy/ns/books/published/dmoi-4/dmoi4.html
  • Open Textbook Library entry: https://open.umn.edu/opentextbooks/textbooks/394
  • LibreTexts mirror: https://math.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Combinatorics_and_Discrete_Mathematics/Discrete_Mathematics_(Levin)

Mathematics for Computer Science — Lehman, Leighton, Meyer (MIT)

The textbook used in MIT's 6.042J course. CC BY-SA. Strong on proofs, induction, graph theory, counting, and discrete probability — with a CS-flavored orientation (state machines, invariants, asymptotics). Excellent companion to the MIT OCW lectures.

  • PDF (author copy): https://people.csail.mit.edu/meyer/mcs.pdf
  • PDF (Spring 2018): https://courses.csail.mit.edu/6.042/spring18/mcs.pdf
  • Internet Archive: https://archive.org/details/MCS_LLM
  • Engineering LibreTexts: https://eng.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Computer_Science/Programming_and_Computation_Fundamentals/Mathematics_for_Computer_Science_(Lehman_Leighton_and_Meyer)

Book of Proof — Richard Hammack

A focused introduction to proof techniques, sets, logic, induction, relations, functions, and infinite cardinality. Approved by AIM's Open Textbook Initiative. Useful as a "bridge" text before or alongside a full discrete math book.

  • Home page: https://richardhammack.github.io/BookOfProof/
  • PDF (3rd ed.): https://richardhammack.github.io/BookOfProof/Main.pdf
  • Open Textbook Library entry: https://open.umn.edu/opentextbooks/textbooks/7

Applied Discrete Structures — Doerr & Levasseur

Classroom-tested open textbook integrating SageMath. Covers sets, counting, logic, matrix algebra, relations, functions, recursion, graphs, trees, algebraic systems (groups, Boolean algebras, monoids), and coding theory.

  • Open Textbook Library entry: https://open.umn.edu/opentextbooks/textbooks/160

Applied Combinatorics — Keller & Trotter

Strong on enumeration, generating functions, recurrence relations, inclusion-exclusion, Polyá theory, posets, graphs, and discrete optimization. A natural follow-up after a first discrete math course.

  • Home page (with HTML, PDF, ePub): https://www.appliedcombinatorics.org/appcomb/

A Spiral Workbook for Discrete Mathematics — Harris Kwong

Sophomore-level: logic, sets, proof techniques, basic number theory, functions, relations, elementary combinatorics. Emphasizes motivation and worked examples.

  • Open SUNY Textbooks: https://textbooks.opensuny.org/a-spiral-workbook-for-discrete-mathematics/

Discrete Mathematics — Levasseur (LibreTexts curated bookshelves)

The LibreTexts Combinatorics and Discrete Mathematics bookshelf aggregates many openly-licensed sources, useful for cross-referencing topics.

  • https://math.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Combinatorics_and_Discrete_Mathematics

Courses and Lecture Notes

Full lecture videos, problem sets, exams, and notes. Pairs directly with the Lehman/Leighton/Meyer textbook above.

  • Fall 2010 (with full video lectures): https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-042j-mathematics-for-computer-science-fall-2010/

Saylor CS202 — Discrete Structures

Free, self-paced course assembled from open materials, with assessments.

  • https://learn.saylor.org/course/view.php?id=67

Public course site with notes and slides on logic, proofs, combinatorics, probability, and graphs.

Suggested Reading Path

  1. Start with Book of Proof (Hammack) for proof fluency, or skip directly to Levin's chapter 1 if comfortable with proofs.
  2. Work through Levin's Discrete Mathematics: An Open Introduction end-to-end as the primary text.
  3. Supplement with Lehman/Leighton/Meyer for CS-flavored treatment of induction, state machines, asymptotics, and probability.
  4. Move to Keller & Trotter's Applied Combinatorics for deeper enumeration, generating functions, and posets.
  5. Use MIT OCW 6.042J videos for any topic where another presentation would help.
