Equivalence Relations

Summary

An equivalence relation on a set $A$ is a relation $R$ that is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive. Part of Functions and Relations.

  • Equivalence classes and quotients
    • The equivalence class of $a$, written $[a]$, is the set ${b \in A : aRb}$.
    • The quotient set of $A$ by $R$, written $A/R$, is the set of all equivalence classes.
  • Partition correspondence
    • The classes of $R$ form a partition of $A$.
    • Conversely, any partition of $A$ induces an equivalence relation whose classes are the parts.

Equivalence relations on $A$ are in bijection with partitions of $A$.

Standard examples include congruence modulo $n$ (see modular_arithmetic) and graph_isomorphism.
