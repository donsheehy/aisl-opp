Indexed Families of Sets

Summary

An indexed family of sets is a collection ${A_i}_{i \in I}$ of sets parameterized by an index set $I$. Part of Set Theory.

  • Generalized unions and intersections
    • The generalized union is $\bigcup_{i \in I} A_i = {x : \exists i \in I, x \in A_i}$.
    • The generalized intersection is $\bigcap_{i \in I} A_i = {x : \forall i \in I, x \in A_i}$.
  • Partitions
    • A partition of $A$ is a family of nonempty, pairwise disjoint subsets of $A$ whose union is $A$.
    • Partitions of $A$ correspond bijectively to equivalence_relations on $A$.
