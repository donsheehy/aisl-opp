Relations

Summary

A binary relation between sets $A$ and $B$ is a subset $R \subseteq A \times B$. Part of Functions and Relations.

  • Properties (for $R \subseteq A \times A$)
    • It is reflexive if $(a, a) \in R$ for every $a \in A$.
    • It is symmetric if $(a, b) \in R$ implies $(b, a) \in R$.
    • It is antisymmetric if $(a, b), (b, a) \in R$ together imply $a = b$.
    • It is transitive if $(a, b), (b, c) \in R$ together imply $(a, c) \in R$.
  • Composition and inverse
    • The composition of $R$ and $S$, written $R \circ S$, is the relation ${(a, c) : \exists b, (a, b) \in S \text{ and } (b, c) \in R}$.
    • The inverse of $R$, written $R^{-1}$, is the relation ${(b, a) : (a, b) \in R}$.

Specializations include equivalence_relations, partial_orders, and functions (the single-valued, total relations).
