Predicate Logic

Summary

Predicate logic (first-order logic) extends propositional_logic by adding predicates, variables, and quantifiers that allow statements about objects in a domain. Part of Logic and Proofs.

  • Quantifiers, scope, free vs. bound variables
    • The universal quantifier $\forall x P(x)$ asserts that $P$ holds for every element $x$ in the domain.
    • The existential quantifier $\exists x P(x)$ asserts that $P$ holds for at least one element $x$.
    • A variable is bound if it occurs within the scope of a quantifier on it; otherwise it is free.
  • Nested quantifiers and quantifier negation
    • Quantifier order matters: $\forall x \exists y P(x,y)$ is generally not equivalent to $\exists y \forall x P(x,y)$.
    • The negation of $\forall x P(x)$ is $\exists x \neg P(x)$, and the negation of $\exists x P(x)$ is $\forall x \neg P(x)$.
