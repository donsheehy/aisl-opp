Discrete Calculus

Summary

Discrete calculus is a finite-difference analogue of differential calculus, especially useful for evaluating summations and solving recurrences. Part of Sequences, Asymptotics, and Discrete Calculus.

  • Finite differences
    • The forward difference operator is $\Delta f(n) = f(n+1) - f(n)$, a linear operator playing the role of differentiation.
  • Falling and rising powers
    • The falling power (also called falling factorial or descending factorial) is $n^{\underline{k}} = n(n-1)\cdots(n-k+1)$, the discrete analogue of $x^k$.
    • The rising power (also called rising factorial) is $n^{\overline{k}} = n(n+1)\cdots(n+k-1)$.
  • Summation by parts
    • Summation by parts is the discrete analogue of integration by parts.

$\Delta n^{\underline{k}} = k \cdot n^{\underline{k-1}}$ — the discrete analogue of the power rule.

Summation by parts: \[ \sum_{n=a}^{b-1} f(n) \Delta g(n) = \Big[f(n) g(n)\Big]{a}^{b} - \sum{n=a}^{b-1} g(n+1) \Delta f(n). \]
