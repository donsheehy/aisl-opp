Graph Coloring

Summary

Graph coloring is the assignment of labels (colors) to the vertices of a graph so that no two adjacent vertices share the same color. Part of Graph Theory.

  • Chromatic number and bounds
    • The chromatic number $\chi(G)$ is the smallest $k$ such that $G$ admits a proper $k$-coloring.
    • A greedy bound gives $\chi(G) \leq \Delta(G) + 1$, where $\Delta(G)$ is the maximum degree.
    • $\chi(G) = 2$ if and only if $G$ is bipartite (see special_graph_classes).
  • Greedy coloring
    • Greedy coloring processes vertices in some order, assigning each the smallest color not used by an already-colored neighbor.
    • The vertex order matters; the greedy algorithm uses at most $\Delta(G) + 1$ colors.
  • Four color theorem
    • Every planar graph has chromatic number at most $4$.
    • The four color theorem was the first major theorem proved with substantial computer assistance (Appel and Haken, 1976).
