Paths and Connectivity

Summary

Paths and connectivity concern the ways of moving through a graph and the resulting reachability structure. Part of Graph Theory.

  • Walks, trails, paths, cycles
    • A walk is an alternating sequence of vertices and edges.
    • A trail is a walk with no repeated edge.
    • A path is a walk with no repeated vertex.
    • A cycle is a closed path with at least three distinct vertices (in a simple graph).
  • Connected components
    • The relation "there is a walk from $u$ to $v$" is an equivalence relation; its classes are the connected components of $G$.
  • Vertex and edge connectivity
    • The vertex connectivity $\kappa(G)$ is the minimum number of vertices whose removal disconnects $G$.
    • The edge connectivity $\lambda(G)$ is the minimum number of edges whose removal disconnects $G$.

Whitney's theorem: $\kappa(G) \leq \lambda(G) \leq \delta(G)$, where $\delta(G)$ is the minimum degree.
