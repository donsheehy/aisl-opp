Sequences

Summary

A sequence is a function from the natural numbers to some set (often $\mathbb{R}$) that describes discrete, ordered data. Part of Sequences, Asymptotics, and Discrete Calculus.

  • Arithmetic and geometric sequences
    • An arithmetic sequence has the form $a_n = a_0 + nd$, where $d$ is the common difference; the sum of the first $n$ terms is \[ \dfrac{n(a_0 + a_{n-1})}{2}. \]
    • A geometric sequence has the form $a_n = a_0 r^n$; its partial sum is \[ a_0\dfrac{1 - r^n}{1 - r} \text{ for } r \neq 1. \]
  • Polynomial and exponential sequences
    • A polynomial sequence is one in which $a_n$ is a polynomial of fixed degree in $n$.
    • An exponential sequence has the form $a_n = c \cdot r^n$ with $|r| > 1$.
    • Exponential growth eventually dominates polynomial growth (see asymptotic_notation).
