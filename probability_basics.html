Probability Basics

Summary

Probability is the mathematical framework for reasoning about random phenomena; on discrete sample spaces it is governed by Kolmogorov's three axioms. Part of Discrete Probability.

  • Sample spaces, events, axioms
    • The sample space $\Omega$ is the set of all possible outcomes of a random experiment.
    • An event is a subset $E \subseteq \Omega$.
    • The probability function $\Pr$ satisfies $\Pr[E] \geq 0$, $\Pr[\Omega] = 1$, and countable additivity for pairwise disjoint events.
  • Uniform probability spaces
    • In a uniform probability space over a finite $\Omega$, every outcome has probability $\Pr[{\omega}] = 1/|\Omega|$.
    • Under uniformity, $\Pr[E] = |E| / |\Omega|$, reducing probability to counting.
