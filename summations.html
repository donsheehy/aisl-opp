Summations

Summary

Summation techniques are methods for evaluating finite and infinite sums in closed form or asymptotically. Part of Sequences, Asymptotics, and Discrete Calculus.

  • Sum manipulation rules
    • Linearity states $\sum (a x_i + b y_i) = a \sum x_i + b \sum y_i$.
    • Reindexing shifts the summation variable: $\sum_{i=a}^{b} f(i) = \sum_{j=0}^{b-a} f(j+a)$.
    • Splitting breaks a sum at an intermediate index: $\sum_{i=a}^{c} = \sum_{i=a}^{b} + \sum_{i=b+1}^{c}$.
  • Closed forms for common sums
    • The sum of the first $n$ positive integers is $\sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \dfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$.
    • The sum of squares is $\sum_{i=1}^{n} i^2 = \dfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$.
    • The geometric sum satisfies $\sum_{i=0}^{n} r^i = \dfrac{1 - r^{n+1}}{1 - r}$ for $r \neq 1$.
  • Perturbation method
    • Extract the first or last term to write $S_n + a_{n+1} = a_0 + \sum_{i=0}^{n} a_{i+1}$.
    • Rewrite the right side to obtain a solvable equation for $S_n$.
