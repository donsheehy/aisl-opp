Recurrence Relations

Summary

A recurrence relation is an equation that defines each term of a sequence in terms of earlier ones. Part of Combinatorics.

  • Linear homogeneous recurrences
    • A linear homogeneous recurrence of order $k$ has the form \[ T(n) = c_1 T(n-1) + \cdots + c_k T(n-k), \] where the coefficients $c_1, \ldots, c_k$ are constants.
    • Such a recurrence is solved by guessing $T(n) = r^n$ and finding the roots of the resulting characteristic polynomial.
  • Linear non-homogeneous recurrences
    • A non-homogeneous recurrence adds a forcing term $F(n)$, giving \[ T(n) = c_1 T(n-1) + \cdots + c_k T(n-k) + F(n). \]
    • The general solution is the sum of the homogeneous solution and any particular solution.
  • Characteristic equations
    • For a recurrence of order $k$, the characteristic polynomial has degree $k$.
    • Distinct roots $r_1, \ldots, r_k$ give \[ T(n) = \sum_i \alpha_i r_i^n; \] repeated roots introduce polynomial factors in $n$.
  • Master theorem (algorithmic context)
    • For divide-and-conquer recurrences of the form $T(n) = aT(n/b) + F(n)$, the master theorem compares $F(n)$ to $n^{\log_b a}$ to give the asymptotic order of $T(n)$.

See also generating_functions for an alternative solution method, and asymptotic_notation for the growth rates that appear in the master theorem.
