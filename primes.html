Primes

Summary

A prime is an integer $p > 1$ whose only positive divisors are $1$ and $p$. Part of Number Theory. Builds on divisibility.

  • Fundamental theorem of arithmetic
    • Every integer $n > 1$ has a unique factorization into primes, up to ordering.
  • Infinitude of primes
    • Given any finite list of primes, one can construct a prime not on the list.
  • Sieve of Eratosthenes
    • The sieve of Eratosthenes enumerates primes up to $n$ by crossing out multiples of each prime up to $\sqrt{n}$, running in $O(n \log \log n)$ time.

Fundamental theorem of arithmetic: every integer $n > 1$ has a unique factorization into primes, up to ordering.

There are infinitely many primes.

Given primes $p_1, \ldots, p_k$, the integer $N = p_1 \cdots p_k + 1$ has a prime factor distinct from each $p_i$.
