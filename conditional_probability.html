Conditional Probability

Summary

Conditional probability is the probability of one event given that another event has occurred. Part of Discrete Probability. Builds on probability_basics.

  • Definition and law of total probability
    • The conditional probability of $A$ given $B$ is $\Pr[A \mid B] = \dfrac{\Pr[A \cap B]}{\Pr[B]}$ when $\Pr[B] > 0$.
    • The law of total probability states: if ${B_i}$ partitions $\Omega$, then \[ \Pr[A] = \displaystyle\sum_i \Pr[A \mid B_i] \Pr[B_i]. \]
  • Bayes' theorem
    • Bayes' theorem inverts conditional probabilities and is foundational to Bayesian inference.
  • Independence of events
    • Events $A$ and $B$ are independent if $\Pr[A \cap B] = \Pr[A] \Pr[B]$, equivalently $\Pr[A \mid B] = \Pr[A]$ when $\Pr[B] > 0$.
    • Mutual independence of a family of events is strictly stronger than pairwise independence.

Bayes' theorem: $\Pr[B \mid A] = \dfrac{\Pr[A \mid B] \Pr[B]}{\Pr[A]}$.
