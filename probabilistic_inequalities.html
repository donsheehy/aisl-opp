Probabilistic Inequalities

Summary

Probabilistic inequalities are tail bounds that estimate the probability that a random variable deviates substantially from its expected value. Part of Discrete Probability.

  • Markov's inequality
    • Markov's inequality bounds the probability that a nonnegative random variable exceeds a threshold, using only the expectation.
  • Chebyshev's inequality
    • Chebyshev's inequality bounds the probability of deviation from the mean in terms of the variance.
  • Chernoff bounds
    • Chernoff bounds give exponentially decaying tail probabilities for sums of independent bounded random variables, via $\Pr[X \geq a] \leq e^{-ta} \mathbb{E}[e^{tX}]$.

Markov's inequality: for nonnegative $X$ and $a > 0$, $\Pr[X \geq a] \leq \dfrac{\mathbb{E}[X]}{a}$.

Chebyshev's inequality: for any $X$ with finite variance, $\Pr[|X - \mathbb{E}[X]| \geq k \sigma_X] \leq \dfrac{1}{k^2}$.
