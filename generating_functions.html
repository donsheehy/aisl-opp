Generating Functions

Summary

A generating function is a formal power series whose coefficients encode a sequence, converting combinatorial problems into algebraic ones. Part of Combinatorics.

  • Ordinary generating functions
    • For a sequence $(a_n)$, the ordinary generating function (OGF) is: \[ A(x) = \sum_{n \geq 0} a_n x^n. \]
    • Operations on series correspond to combinatorial operations: addition, product, shift, and differentiation.
  • Exponential generating functions
    • The exponential generating function (EGF) is: \[ A(x) = \sum_{n \geq 0} a_n \frac{x^n}{n!}. \]
    • This form is well-suited to labeled structures; the product of two EGFs corresponds to merging labeled sets.
  • Solving recurrences via generating functions
    • Multiply the recurrence by $x^n$ and sum over all $n$ to obtain an algebraic equation for $A(x)$.
    • Solve for $A(x)$, then extract the coefficients using partial fractions or known series expansions.
