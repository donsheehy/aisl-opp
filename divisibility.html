Divisibility

Summary

Divisibility is the relation in which one integer is an exact multiple of another: we say $b$ divides $a$, written $b \mid a$, if there exists an integer $k$ such that $a = bk$. Part of Number Theory.

  • Division algorithm
    • For integers $a$ and $b > 0$, there exist unique integers $q$ (the quotient) and $r$ (the remainder) satisfying $a = bq + r$ and $0 \leq r < b$.
  • Greatest common divisor and least common multiple
    • The greatest common divisor $\gcd(a, b)$ and the least common multiple $\mathrm{lcm}(a, b)$ are computed via the Euclidean algorithm.
    • The extended Euclidean algorithm produces Bézout coefficients $x, y$ satisfying $ax + by = \gcd(a, b)$.

For integers $a$ and $b > 0$, there exist unique integers $q, r$ with $a = bq + r$ and $0 \leq r < b$.

Foundation for gcd, primes, and modular_arithmetic.
