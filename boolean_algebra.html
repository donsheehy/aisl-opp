Boolean Algebra

Summary

Boolean algebra is the algebra of two-valued logic, with operations and identities governing the manipulation of truth values. Part of Algebraic Structures.

  • Axioms and identities
    • The operations $\wedge$, $\vee$, and $\neg$ on ${0, 1}$ satisfy associativity, commutativity, distributivity, identity elements $0$ and $1$, and complement laws.
    • Duality: any identity remains valid when $\wedge$ and $\vee$ are swapped and $0$ and $1$ are swapped.
  • Boolean functions and circuits
    • A Boolean function is a function $f : {0,1}^n \to {0,1}$.
    • Boolean functions are realized by logic gates ($\mathrm{AND}$, $\mathrm{OR}$, $\mathrm{NOT}$); the set ${\mathrm{NAND}}$ alone is functionally complete.
    • Boolean algebra connects directly to propositional_logic.
  • Simplification (Karnaugh maps)
    • A Karnaugh map is a graphical method for minimizing Boolean expressions of up to four to six variables.
    • Groups of adjacent 1-cells correspond to product terms in a minimal sum-of-products form.
