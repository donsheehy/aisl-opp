Invariants and Correctness

Summary

Invariants are properties preserved throughout a computation; they are the primary tool for proving correctness of programs and processes. Part of Discrete Structures for Computation.

  • Loop invariants
    • A loop invariant is a property $P$ that holds before each iteration of a loop.
    • To prove $P$ is an invariant: show $P$ holds initially and that one iteration preserves $P$.
    • Combined with the loop exit condition, $P$ implies the post-condition correctness of the loop.
  • State-machine invariants
    • In a state machine, a preserved invariant is a property $P$ such that $P(q)$ and $q \to q'$ together imply $P(q')$.
    • If $P$ holds at the initial state, it holds at every reachable state.
  • Termination via well-founded orderings
    • A well-founded ordering is one with no infinite descending chains.
    • Exhibit a function from states to a well-founded set that strictly decreases at each step to guarantee termination.
    • The well-ordering principle is the prototype.
