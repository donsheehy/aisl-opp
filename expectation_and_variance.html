Expectation and Variance

Summary

The expected value and variance are the primary summary statistics of a random variable, measuring its center and spread. Part of Discrete Probability.

  • Expected value
    • The expected value (or mean) of a discrete random variable is $\mathbb{E}[X] = \displaystyle\sum_x x \cdot \Pr[X = x]$.
    • Linearity of expectation states $\mathbb{E}[aX + bY] = a \mathbb{E}[X] + b \mathbb{E}[Y]$ for any random variables $X, Y$ and constants $a, b$, with no independence assumption required.
  • Variance and standard deviation
    • The variance of $X$ is $\mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mathbb{E}[X])^2] = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - (\mathbb{E}[X])^2$.
    • The standard deviation is $\sigma_X = \sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(X)}$.
  • Covariance and correlation
    • The covariance of $X$ and $Y$ is $\mathrm{Cov}(X, Y) = \mathbb{E}[XY] - \mathbb{E}[X]\mathbb{E}[Y]$.
    • The correlation is $\rho(X,Y) = \dfrac{\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y} \in [-1, 1]$.
    • If $X$ and $Y$ are independent then $\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) = 0$, but the converse fails in general.

Linearity of expectation: for any random variables $X, Y$ and constants $a, b$, $\mathbb{E}[aX + bY] = a \mathbb{E}[X] + b \mathbb{E}[Y]$, with no independence assumption.
