Propositional Logic

Summary

Propositional logic is the branch of mathematical logic that studies propositions—atomic true/false statements—combined via logical connectives. Part of Logic and Proofs.

  • Connectives, truth tables, tautologies, satisfiability
    • The standard connectives are negation $\neg$, conjunction $\wedge$, disjunction $\vee$, implication $\rightarrow$, and biconditional $\leftrightarrow$.
    • A tautology is a formula that is true under every truth assignment.
    • A formula is satisfiable if some truth assignment makes it true.
  • Logical equivalence and normal forms
    • Two formulas $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are logically equivalent, written $\varphi \equiv \psi$, if they agree on every truth assignment.
    • Conjunctive normal form (CNF) expresses a formula as a conjunction of disjunctions of literals.
    • Disjunctive normal form (DNF) expresses a formula as a disjunction of conjunctions of literals.
